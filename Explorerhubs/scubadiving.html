SCUBA DIVING



Best Places for Scuba Diving in India for Adventure Destination


Scuba Diving

Scuba diving is a mode of underwater diving whereby divers use breathing equipment that is completely independent of a surface air supply, and therefore has a limited but variable endurance. The name “scuba”, an acronym for “Self-Contained Underwater Breathing Apparatus. To dive into the deep oceans and to explore the world beneath is unexplainable in words. And to experience something like this, you need to go scuba diving in India.

Scuba diving may be done recreationally or professionally in a number of applications, including scientific, military and public safety roles, but most commercial diving uses surface-supplied diving equipment when this is practicable. Scuba divers engaged in armed forces covert operations may be referred to as frogmen, combat divers or attack swimmers.

Why Scuba Diving in India?

Scuba diving in India comes with oodles of fun and frolic. Countless species of fish and other marine animals and the colourful underwater reefs add life to the oceans underwater. The best places to dive in India are Lakshadweep and Andaman and Nicobar are the places to get a world-class diving experience followed by Dwarka and the coastline of Karnataka. It attempts to give you all a brief insight into what to expect while diving and the best spots to get that experience in India.

  • Havelock Island – Andaman and Nicobar Island
  • Neil Island – Andaman and Nicobar Island
  • Kovalam – Kerala
  • North Point, Cinque Island, Andaman and Nicobar Islands

Havelock Island – Andaman and Nicobar Island

Andaman islands should be on top of every list related to beaches, sea, untouched beauty, and scuba diving in India. Andaman is one of the amazing creations of the Almighty. Situated right in the middle of the Indian Ocean and Bay of Bengal, this archipelago has a number of stunningly beautiful beaches and scuba diving spots, Havelock Islands being one of the most popular. Located about 50 km away from Port Blair, the island has a wide range of exotic marine life like Humpback Parrotfish, Lionfish, soft corals, The rare dugongs and schools of colourful fishes. There are many interesting sites upon reaching there like there is The Wall, Lighthouse, Aquarium, and Mac Point etc each with its own unique features. The Bay of Bengal offers an amazing underwater life to witness. The coral reefs make an ideal atmosphere for this fun activity.



Duration:

2 hrs 30 minutes

Location:

Ladakh

Best time to go Scuba Diving in Havelock Island – Andaman and Nicobar Island:

October-April

Best diving sites:

Turtle Bay, The wall, lighthouse, aquarium and Dixon’s Pinnacle.

Best organizers:

Barefoot Scuba, India Scuba Explorers, Eco Diver, Dive India Outbound, Ocean Tribe Scuba.

Cost:

INR 4500-INR 6000

Neil Island – Andaman and Nicobar Island

This is another gem of Andaman which is beautiful in a subtle way and also away from the chaos of the main town. It is a tiny island but a really beautiful one with its yet unexplored beaches. Neil island is an isolated place which is known for its unexplored and hidden beaches which also offers a great scuba diving spot. The coral reef here is quite untouched by external factors and hence retains its beauty owing to the rich variety of marine life. Preserves its rich and diverse beauty of flora and fauna. Neil Island is certainly amongst the best places for underwater diving in India. The coral reef is still untouched and preserves its rich and diverse beauty of flora and fauna.




Duration:

2 hrs 30 minutes

Best time to go for Scuba Diving in Neil Island, Andaman and Nicobar Island:

December-April

Best Diving Sites:

Jetty area, Margherita's Mischief and Laxmanpur Beach.

Cost:

INR 3500-INR 6000

Best Organizers:

K Rock, Bus Stop, Margherita’s Mischief

Kovalam – Kerala

Kovalam is becoming a famous spot for scuba diving in India amongst scuba divers. Kovalam is situated in Kerala, at a distance of about 16 km from Thiruvananthapuram. A beach area mainly, it is slowly developing into a popular scuba diving locale since the Bond Safari Kovalam has come there. The water is clean and offers a glimpse into a rich sea-life with lots of sea and creatures. Moreover the waters here are really calm, with shoals of anchovy and silver moony, Bluefin trevaly, the odd Moray eel, puffer fish, groupers, box fish, sergeants, pipe fish etc. The place is well connected by railways and airports. Kovalam is one of the most unique scuba diving destinations in India.



Duration:

2 hrs 30 minutes

Best time to go for Scuba Diving in Kovalam, Kerala:

September to February

Best Diving Sites:

Lighthouse Beach, Hawah Beach Cost: INR 4500 per person (the rates may vary)

Best Organizers:

Bond safari, scuba cochin dive centre

North Point, Cinque Island, Andaman & Nicobar Islands

Another beauty of the Andaman and Nicobar islands, this spot has one of the clearest waters in the world. When it comes to exploring the islands in Andaman, Cinque island is also one of the magnificent islands to visit and explore. Visitors can see as deep as 80 meters below sea level with naked eyes, which means it is one of the best places for scuba diving in India. The place has a variety of amazing black corals and also ferocious sharks. After reaching Port Blair, one can go to Cinque Island by a chartered boat.




Duration:

2 hrs 30 minutes

Best time to go for Scuba Diving in North Point, Cinque Island, Andaman and Nicobar Island:

December-May

Best Diving Sites:

NA

Best organizers:

Barefoot Scuba

Cost:

INR 3500-INR 5000

Grand Island, Goa

Goa is a popular destination for travelers but it is also famous for scuba diving in India. Goa offers sheer happiness with tiny exotic corals, attractive fishes, and other exotic marine life. The Grand Island is a popular destination for adventure junkies all over the world. Located few kilometers westwards of the Mormugao peninsula, this place is a haven for scuba divers.

Amongst the most popular sites is the Suzy’s Wreck, a British ship sunken in the 1930’s due to strong storm. The ship still remains at the bottom covered with mussels and corals, becoming a home for many fishes and crustaceans There is another shipwreck called Davy Jones Locker not far away, which is a favorite among the professional scuba divers. Goa one of the safest options for scuba diving in India.



Duration:

2 hrs 30 minutes

Best time to go for Scuba Diving in Grand Island – Goa :

October to mid May

Cost:

INR 5000 per person, which includes training and one dive

Best Organizers:

Barracuda Diving, Dive Goa and Elan Adventures

Essentials for Scuba Diving:

Scuba diving is undoubtedly a popular ocean activity, especially for those who love to explore the hidden beauty of the ocean. However, it requires quite a bit of preparation. But most importantly, you will need to equip yourself with the right scuba diving equipment.


Equipment list for Scuba Diving

  • Sunblock
  • Fins
  • Scuba Gloves
  • Regulator
  • Scuba tank
  • Dive computer
  • Buoyancy Compensator
  • Snorkel
  • Underwater camera
  • Diving mask
  • Rehydrating Salt
  • Dive Logbook
  • Dry Bag
  • Quality Diving Footwear
  • Depth Gauge, Compass, Submersible Pressure Gauge
  • A Good Quality Wetsuit
