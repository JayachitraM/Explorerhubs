Best Places for Mountain Biking in India for Adventure Destination


Mountain Biking

Mountain biking is the sport of riding bicycles off-road. Often this is done over rough terrain. Mountain biking uses specially designed mountain bikes. Mountain bikes are similar to other bikes, but have features designed to make them stronger and hold up in rough terrain.

Why Mountain Biking in India?

Mountain Biking in India is the answer for vacations with a fun frolic agenda with your group of wanderers. When you get close to nature while mountain riding across rough off-road terrain, the sense of being superman is unrivalled. There are numerous mountain bike adventure sites in India, and you can go sightseeing in and around them. It is a kind of adventure sport which can be enjoyed far off the roads, often of rugged hills and terrains. India caters to all the needs one is looking for to get out of the city life and indulge into nature’s beauty. Solitude and mountain biking in India is pretty much the same thing! Do you want to experience the joy of wandering in the best places for mountain biking in India? If yes, then embark on a biking expedition and satiate your wanderlust soul.




Here we have best 5 places In India for an amazing mountain biking experiences

Manali-Leh

The Manali to Leh Mountain bike trip route in the Himalayas is famous not only for its remarkable natural beauty but also for mystic Buddhist architecture and trekking areas. It has all the kind of terrains for the riders who want big mountain riding or the ones who want challenging climbing ride and a lot more like that. Starting from Manali, the route stretches over the Himalayas and spans up to Khardungla- the highest motorable road in the world. The distance between Manali to Leh is 427.6 Kms. A non-stop drive will take about 11.5 hours to reach. Dare to complete it at least once in a lifetime and live to narrate the stories of real thrill.

11-13 days

June – September

Sikkim, North East India

Explore the most popular mountain biking trails in Sikkim with hand-curated trail maps and driving directions as well as detailed reviews. The rugged and dramatic terrain of Sikkim offers sure and exciting biking trails to bikers and adventure seekers around the world. The longest mountain biking trail in Sikkim is Ribdi MTB Loop. Shimla to Spiti Valley

One of the most awe-inspiring mountain biking trails in India. You know about Shimla that it is the ‘white winter Queen of India,’ but what about Spiti Valley? Considered next to Ladakh when it comes to natural beauty and awe-inspiring roads, Spiti Valley the getaway into the country’s higher northern reaches. Taking a motorbike to Spiti has been a pretty common adventure now, hence for those who seek to get off the beaten path should probably give mountain biking a try. Embark on a joyous ride to Spiti Valley from Shimla. Even if you are an amateur bike rider, the route we suggested will help you ascend gradually without the risk of Acute Mountain Sickness. You will come across a bunch of beautiful places from Shimla to the Spiti Valley route, including Narkanda, Sarahan, Chitkul, Kalpa, Reckong Peo, Sangla, Nako Village, Tabo Village, Dhankar, and Kaza. Essentials for Mountain Biking:

Getting ready to hit the dirt? This comprehensive list for mountain bikers is designed to prevent you from forgetting anything important on your next ride. Mountain bike accessories that are essential to Always bring on a ride.


1.Helmet

Even if you have cat-like reflexes on a bike, always landing rubber side down, you NEED to rock a helmet. With that being said, in most situations you don’t need a full-face helmet unless you’re doing full-on downhill or aggressive enduro riding. Nearly every mountain bike helmet on the market comes with one. It serves two purposes. One is to provide a bit of shade against sun rays and help provide better vision. The second is to give your face some protection from low hanging branches and other debris.


2.Tubeless repair kit

Tubeless plugs are the mountain biker’s best friend, and can be easily applied to a hole in the tyre using a specialist plug tool to seal the hole ready for reinflation. One of the most common mechanical issues on the trail is a puncture, Larger cuts or slices might not work with plugs, so you’ll need to remove the tyre to install a tyre boot and tube. Many brands now offer complete kits for tubeless repairs, including reamers, plugs and application tools.


3.Spare tubes

If your tubeless nightmare is beyond repair, or if you need to fix a punctured inner tube, it’s probably time to pop a replacement tube in there. They may be a little bulky, but it really does pay to carry spare inner tubes, even if you have your tyres set up tubeless. It may sound like a lot, but for big rides it’s well worth taking two spare tubes, as double flats do happen.


4. Patch kit

These small kits can be a lifesaver when it comes to being able to reuse a punctured tube, and can sometimes even be used for other applications such as patching the inside of a tyre. Worthy of a spot in your ride kit, the humble tube-patching kit is an essential regardless of your tyre setup.


5.Mini Tyre pump

Mini hand pump though a pump can be used over and over again which, alas, may come in handy. Whether you’ve ‘burped’ a bit of pressure out of your tyres or have had a puncture, a tyre pump is key to getting you back to that sweet-running optimum. Look for a high-volume option for mountain bike tyres, rather than a high-pressure pump which works better for narrower road bike tyres. Whereas a pump can be used again and again for years to come.
