Best Places for Paragliding in France for Adventure Destination


Paragliding

Paragliding is a sport in which the players fly in the air using paragliders. It’s one of the rawest and most pure forms of flight. Paragliding demands jumping from high heels and to ski in the air for long hours. France has a multitude of perfect paragliding spots, from the snow-capped peaks of the French Alps, to the beaches of Normandy. This stunning country has a wide variety of paragliding and tandem paragliding experiences to offer everyone, from experienced paragliders to complete beginners to the sport.

Why do people choose Paragliding?

Paragliding is considered a safe activity, but there are some risks involved. Flying in smooth weather on a paraglider can be surprisingly calm and relaxing. Of course, if you want a more exciting flight, there are ways of making that happen. People are often surprised by how comfortable they feel in the air and love how they feel “at one” with the skies.

Annecy

Annecy, city, capital of Haute-Savoie departments, Auvergne-Rhône-Alpes region, southeastern France. Annecy is internationally known for the beauty of its harmonious landscapes, but also for the quality of the lake water. This stunning lakeside city in southeastern France is a paragliding haven, with tourists flocking from the world-over to get in on the action. With a number of different flight types, routes and lengths on offer at this location and paragliding training also available, Annecy has an air sport experience for everyone! Located just under an hour’s drive from the Swiss city of Geneva and on the edge of the French Alps, Annecy is a well-located and stunning spot for paragliding. Annecy and is a world-famous take-off location.

45 minutes and more

Height:

2,297 ft

Best time to go Paragliding in Annecy:

September-October

Cost:

From 115€ - 20 à 30 minutes and From 125€ - 20 to 30 minutes

Best organizers:

Saint Hilaire,Grenoble

This destination is a world famous spot in the south-east of France, known in the paragliding world for being the host-town of the Coupe Icare. Located near Grenoble in the Chartreuse mountains, the village of Saint Hilaire du Touvet welcomes paragliders from all over the world! Since 1976 and the 1st edition of the Coupe Icare, the reputation of the site is well known. This annual free flying event held in St Hilaire and the village of Lumbin, in the valley below, brings paragliding enthusiasts from all over the world. This 8 day annual festival, held in this area since 1974, offers visitors four scenic take off areas, near the cliff top village of Saint Hilaire, and a landing area 750m below in Lumbin. Saint Hilaire is a super easy ride from Grenoble, taking just a 25km drive to reach this famous paragliding place in France.





Duration:

5 days

Best time to go for Paragliding in Saint Hilaire,Grenoble :

April, May and June

Cost:

€670/person

Best organizers:

Dune Du Pilat

Dune du Pilat is a fabulous natural landmark that is well known amongst travelers, due to the paraglider’s favorite optimal winds soaring above the dunes and the coastlines. The Dune Is 2,500m long and about 500m wide. It sits smack in the middle of France’s west coast, 10km from the surfer town of Arcachon and 70km from Bordeaux. The Dune is divided into three sections: “the large dune”, “the centre of the dune” and “the south of the dune”. A naturally occurring sand wall, 65km southwest of Bordeaux, nestled along the coast of the Bay of Biscay is the ideal place in France for paragliding.



Duration:

30 minutes

Best time to go forParagliding in Dune Du Pilat:

April to October

Cost:

€220/person

Best organizers:

Dune Paragliding

Puy de Dôme

Paragliding in France can’t be completed without this thriller flight. Paraglide from the top of the 10,000 year old lava dome, Puy de Dôme. Gain stunning views of the surrounding lush green countryside and of the other domes, or dormant volcanoes, in the Chaine des Puys volcanic chain, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It is above all a site where you can take off from any direction! And that’s a big advantage in paragliding. If the weather is good and the wind is not too strong, you can fly to the top. Taking 25 minutes to drive from the city of Clermont-Ferrand and a two- hour’s drive from the largest city of Lyon, it is a perfect spot to visit. You can even try paragliding at the Dune du Pilat with certain paragliding structures.




Duration:

30 minutes

Best time to go for Paragliding in Puy de Dôme:

March to September

Cost:

€120/person

Best organizers:

Absolute Paragliding Puy De Dome

Chamonix

The Chamonix-Mont-Blanc Valley is an iconic destination for paragliders. Chamonix is a place where you can practice paragliding. Its take-off areas are easy to reach with the ski lifts and the landscapes are breathtaking – a magical experience for free-flight enthusiasts. PlanPraz is the most famous site in Chamonix. Facing the Mont-Blanc, you can make some nice flights, 1000 meters above the Chamonix valley. It’s a site that quickly becomes thermal during the day and requires some experience and technique. All the major paragliding sites in France, you will find structures to help you discover paragliding in a tandem flight for non-practitioners who wish to discover the sensations of paragliding. This is not the main paragliding site in France, nor the one that offers the most facilities, but it is a truly magnificent site for the scenery facing Mont Blanc!




Duration:

20-30 minutes

Best time to go for Paragliding in Chamonix:

:April to September

Cost:

16 euros and 18.5 euros

Best organizers:

Absolute Chamonix – Parapente
