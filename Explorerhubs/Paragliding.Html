Paragliding in India

Paragliding is the recreational and competitive adventure sport of flying paragliders. It is lightweight, free-flying, foot- launched glider aircraft with no rigid primary structure. The pilot sits in a harness or lies supine in a cocoon like ‘pod' suspended below a fabric wing. It is a motor free sport practised by the adventurous people. It is weather dependent adventure aviation sport, done by trained pilots either solo or with passenger. Paragliding wings are specially designed aerofoil shape canopies called “paraglider". The paraglider wing is the most important element of paragliding equipment and it consists of two main layers, top and bottom surfaces. These, in turn, are joined by a fabric called ribs — forming between them what we call “cells” or “boxes”. Arm strength is obviously important to steer the glider, but you also need lower body strength, because to accelerate you have to push on a speed bar with your legs. You also need good neck strength, because you lie down to be more aerodynamic and the neck has a lot of forces on it. In paragliding launching and the landing is done with the wind. The wind is used as an airstream by running, being pulled or with the help of existing wind. The pilots are moved in a place from where they can be lifted. There are three different launching techniques: Forward Launch, Reverse Launch, and Towed Launch.
Age limit: There is no age limit for paragliding.


Benefits of paragliding

Paragliding helps to build a strong core, which helps with balance, posture, and stability. This aerial sport engages your deep core muscles, which strengthens your body so that it also reduces the risk of injury during exercises and activities.

Risks in paragliding

  • Injuries from crashes
  • Getting lost or stranded in remote areas
  • Exposure to bad weather

Bir Billing- Himachal Pradesh

Bir billing, Kangara district, Himachal Pradesh. It is called the Paragliding capital of India. Bir and billing are two separate villages, Bir is landing site for paragliding and billing is the take off location for paragliding. The distance between Bir and Billing is about 15 km.



Speed limit:

20-75 kmph (12-47 mph)

Weight limit:

In Bir Billing there is a weight limit for paasengers between 15 to 95 kg. Infact the weight is between 95 to 115 kg, there is a extra fee will be charged for Rs.500. Kids must have minimum weight of 30kg.

Best time to go Bungee jumping in Bir Billing- Himachal Pradesh:

March to June and October to November

Minimum age:

12 years

Cost:

Two types of paragliding. (The price is same for all age groups)

  • Short paragliding fly consists of 15-20 mins duration is around Rs.3000 per person.
  • Long paragliding fly consists of 20-30 mins duration is around Rs.4000 per person.

    • Best organizers:

    Camp Oak View, Friends adventures, and tours, Blue Umbrella

    Manali-Himachal Pradesh

    Manali is a beautiful valley located in the heart of the himalayas. This town is located at an altitude of about 5500ft to 6000ft above the sea level which makes it ideal for paragliding trips. The areas of Solang valley, Fatru mountain, Gulaba, Nagger road are the places where paragliding takes place.



    Best time to go for Paragliding in Manali-Himachal Pradesh:

    October to May

    Cost:

    Starting from Rs.1800 and above.

    Best organizers:

    Ski Himalayas, TigerEye Adventures, Himalayan Extreme centre.

    Kamshet- Maharashtra

    A 100kms from Mumbai and 50kms from Pune, Kamshet is one of the best places for paragliding in the western region of India. Group flying and solo flying are both popular here. The Sahyadri ranges below make for a good sight from up in the skies.



    Best time to go for Paragliding in Kamshet- Maharashtra:

    October to June

    Cost:

    Rs.2500 for tandem flights and above.

    Best organizers:

    Indus Paragliding school, Paragliding Mantra.

    Vagamon-Kerala

    Vagamon is located in the Idukki district, Kerala, nearest to Cochin at 75km distance. This beautiful hill station is a dream for adventure enthusiasts. Here temperature ranging from 10°-23°C on summer time is ideal for paragliding. Also trained pilots are available that means tandem flights are possible.


    Best time to go for Paragliding in Vagamon-Kerala:

    October to March.

    Cost:

    Rs.3500(approx)

    Best organizers:

    Paragliding Kerala


    back to top

    Yelagiri-Tamil Nadu

    Yelagiri is a hill station located in the new formed Tirupattur district of Tamil Nadu. Situated off the Vaniyambadi- Tirupattur road. It located at an altitude of 1111m above sea level and spread across 30kmsq. This hills is surrounded by orchards, rose-gardens, green valleys. Yelagiri is a branch of Annamalai hills. This is one of the best places for paragliding in South India. This hill station also has a paragliding club which lends various paragliding equipment and provides various tandem flights. The beautiful green grass of the hill station and the altitude of about 2000m is perfect for an amazing paragliding experience.


    Best time to go for Paragliding in Yelagiri-Tamil Nadu:

    September to February

    Cost:

    Tandem flights starts from Rs.2000 (approx)

    Best organizers:

    Yelagiri Marigold Ridge.
